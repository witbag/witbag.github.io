Visas

  • These are some rough notes about getting visas on the road (outside your home country). I mainly list fees and processing times.
  • Not reliable info. Use it at your own risk.
  • Requirements/fees may vary depending on your nationality or the mood of the embassy worker.
  • Prices in USD.
  • If you have a "third world" passport, you likely need a visa for almost every country. However, some countries allow visa-free entry if you have a visa for the US, the EU, or a few other countries. See below:
    https://www.visatraveler.com/blog/travel-20-countries-visa-free-with-us-visa/
    https://www.visatraveler.com/blog/travel-non-schengen-countries-visa-free-with-schengen-visa/

Many countries want you to have a return flight and a hotel reservation to apply for visa, even if you intend to travel by land. Even if you don't need a visa you might still get asked to show a return flight at the airport and you won't be allowed to fly without one. To get a "fake" flight itinerary (at your own risk):

  • Use a booking website that lets you choose bank transfer or payment at airline office as payment method (fareboom.com, KLM, Emirates, yatra.com(?)). You will get a booking number that you can use on the airline's website to find your reservation. It will be valid for a few days and cancel automatically if you don't complete the payment.
  • Buy a refundable ticket directly from an airline's website, e.g. a business class ticket. Make sure cancellation is 100% free.
  • US-based booking websites, e.g. expedia.com, orbitz.com (must use their US website with .com domian) offer free cancellation within 24 hours.
  • "Fake" hotel booking: many websites offer free cancellation.

USA
160$ 10-year B visa.
Warning (2019): The USA has the most ridiculous visa requirements in the world, even requiring your social media accounts, as well as past email addresses and phone numbers. You must attend a visa interview. The USA has a list of countries it does not like (currently Iran, Iraq, Libya, Somalia, Sudan, Syria, Yemen, North Korea). If you come from, or even visited any of these countries, you may be refused a visa. The fee is non-refundable. People flying into the US can get interrogated for hours at the airport, causing them to miss their flights.
