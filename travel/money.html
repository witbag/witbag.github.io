Handling money:
  • Find a bank that has no international ATM fees (ING, Citibank in Australia, many credit unions in the USA).
  • Some ATMs will ask if you wish to be charged in local currency or in your bank's currency. Choose local currency to avoid horrible rates.
  • Avoid exchanging cash in Western countries (bad rates). Some countries have "black market" rates which are better than official ones (100 USD bills get best rates).
  • Keep coins and small bills for daily expenses seperate from large bills and other valuables (e.g. in a different pocket).
  • Wallets are easily spotted by thieves. Don't carry any large money in a wallet.
  • Usually it is safer to leave valuable items at the hotel than to carry them with you.
  • Keep some emergency cash somewhere well hidden, just in case.
Some tips on saving money:
  • Be stingy in expensive countries, not in cheap countries (especially if you live in an expensive country).
  • Avoid staying in touristy areas.
  • If you live in an expensive city, renting out your room while you're away could save enough money to cover your entire trip.
  • If you are a student, a teacher, or under 31, get an ISIC/ITIC/IYTC card. It includes travel insurance and some discounts. Costs about 25$.
