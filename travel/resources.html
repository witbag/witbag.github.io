Travel Websites

General planning:
Wikitravel, Wikivoyage, Lonely Planet: Free online guides
Thorn Tree forum: Travel forum
UNESCO World Heritage Sites (a more detailed map here)
Sightsmap: Photographed places (useful when you zoom in closely). Similar map for sports activities.
Dots on Maps: Maps of places to visit.
Climate Maps: Monthly climate maps (also here)
Windy: Weather forecast maps
Airly: Air quality map (also here, here, here, here, here)
Summitpost: Hiking and climbing
Wikiloc: Hiking trails
The Hiking Life: Good hiking blog (except the super expensive "gear" recommendations)
Mapy: Maps
Opentopomap: Topographic maps
Topographic maps
Hikebikemap
Soviet military topographic maps, more detailed than most internet maps (also here, here, here)
Perry–Castañeda Library Map Collection: Old topographic maps. More here, here, here.
Happycow: Map of vegetarian food locations.
Falling Fruit: Freegan map.
Dumpstermap: Dumpster diving map
Visa requirements on Wikipedia: See your country's page for a list of countries for which you need a visa (also here)
Lonely Planet maps: Maps from Lonely Planet guidebooks (without legends).
Library Genesis: For finding travel guide ebooks (also here and here)
Travelpod: Used to be the best resource about obscure places until it got bought and shut down by the evil Tripadvisor.

Transport:
Rome2rio: Find bus, train, and ferry connections
Seat 61: Information about train travel
ÖPNVKarte: Maps of public transport routes
Hitchwiki: Hitchhiking guide. See their maps of hitchhiking locations (also here and here).
Flightconnections: Airline route maps (also here).
Azair: The best search engine for low-cost airlines, but has bugs and is not reliable outside Europe.
Skiplagged: Flights search engine. Good for finding "hidden-city" flights (mainly in the US)
Skyscanner: Flights search engine. Changing the country and currency sometimes gives lower prices.
Kayak: Flights search engine
Fly4free: Blog featuring cheap flight deals
