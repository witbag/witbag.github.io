- Free BBQ hot plates in parks and on beaches.

CHINA
- https://www.chinabusguide.com/
- https://www.trip.com/
- https://map.baidu.com/
- https://www.travelchinaguide.com/cityguides/
- Due to strict censorship it is often hard to find reliable information about China. Both pro-government and anti-government propaganda is widespread and can be impossible to verify, while foreign journalists get expelled.
- Many websites and nearly all foreign apps are banned. Get a VPN or a anti-censorship tool like Psiphon or Ultrasurf before going to China but internet will be extremely slow.
- Traditional architecture is either bulldozed or turned into commercial tourist traps. Historical places have been "renovated" in recent years into Disneyesque amusement parks.
- You pay a steep enterance fee for pretty much every tourist attraction, even mountains, lakes and villages.
- Independent travel is not allowed in Tibet.
- News: https://www.scmp.com/, https://supchina.com/
- Must-try food: huoguo (hot pot). Every region has its own unique cuisine. Vegetarian: 素

ETHIOPIA
- http://www.ethiopia.travel
- Long-distance buses all leave around 5-6 AM. Book 1-2 days in advance.
- Ethiopian 12-hour clock system follows sunrise and sunset (Ethiopian 1 o'clock in the morning = 7 AM, Ethiopian 12 o'clock at night = 6 AM).
- A paid guide is mandatory in national parks.
- Must-try food: injera, salads

- Free mountain huts in some countries (see links above).
- Western Europe pretends to care about the environment, but train travel is extremely expensive (maybe most in the world), while flying can be ridiculously cheap due to various subsidies. Buses are reasonably priced.
- In many train systems, you must "validate" your ticket by putting it into a small machine before boarding the train.
- Low-cost airlines have strict luggage rules. If you forget to check in online you will pay a huge fee.
- Ferries to Africa (under 50 euro): Spain to Morocco, Ceuta, Melilla; Italy to Tunisia.
- Visa rules are insane, while attempting to enter without a visa oftern results in death at sea or wasting years of your life in a concentration camp.
- "Ethnological" museums, especially in London, Paris, Berlin, Brussels, Vienna shamelessly display stolen art from all over the world and refuse to return them.
- You need money to use public toilets, even in bus and train stations.
- Must-try food: cheese in the West, rye bread in the East.

- Buying a train ticket is difficult: the queues are huge, you need to fill out a complicated form, tickets sell out early. Buy tickets online if possible. "Tatkal" are reserve tickets that are more expensive but can be bought after normal tickets have sold out. Buses are far less confusing and more reliable, but slower.
- There is some complicated procedure for checking in foreigners, so many hotels don't accept foreign guests.
- Bring a printed flight reservation to the airport or you will not be allowed inside (never mind that anyone could make a fake one).
- Food in India gets labeled with green (vegetarian) and brown (non-vegetarian) dot symbols.
- News: https://www.thehindu.com/
- Must-try food: thali, desserts, lassi

NEW ZEALAND
- http://www.topomap.co.nz/
- https://www.doc.govt.nz/map/index.html
- https://climbnz.org.nz/
- http://camping_nz.rankers.co.nz/filters/campgrounds
- https://www.google.com/maps/d/viewer?mid=1HDtmvU4XemdY0bDLDfNdSEUnjb0
- https://www.southernalpsphotography.com/
- Camping in national parks is allowed if you get at least 500 m away from a trail.
- "Great Walks": The rangers will insist that you must complete them in several days and stay at the pre-booked huts, but it is perfectly legal to do the treks in a single day.
- Most free campsites and shelters are only for people who come in a big, polluting campervan. Hikers can get fined if they try to sleep there.
- New Zealand is very strict about what food you're allowed to bring into the country.

PERU
- https://www.redbus.pe/en/
- http://www.andeantravelweb.com/peru/
- https://www.peru.travel
- iPeru are the official tourist offices, provide free maps.
- Machu Picchu enterance tickets (must buy in advance): https://www.machupicchu.gob.pe/inicio. Cheap transport option: bus Cusco to Hidroelectrica, then walk along the railway track to Aguas Calientes (main base for MP).
- Free and less touristy alternatives to the expensive Inca Trail: Lares trek, Salkantay trek, Choquequirao trek.
- Yurimaguas is the farthest place connected by road from where you can take a passenger boat to Iquitos and Brazil.
