AUSTRALIA

BELGIUM
- One of the worst mass-murderers in history, Leopold II, is still celerated. There are statues of him all over the country.

BRAZIL
- https://www.buscaonibus.com.br/en
- https://www.blablacar.com.br
- There are regular passenger boats on the major rivers in the Amazon, also connecting to Peru. Bring a hammock.

CAMBODIA
- https://www.camboticket.com/
- https://bookmebus.com/en
- Train: http://www.easybook.com/
- US Dollar is the most common currency (available from ATMs). Cambodian riel is used mainly as small change.
- Must-try food: amok, kralan

CANADA
- https://freecampsites.net/
- Western half of Canada has almost no inter-city public transport.

CHINA
- https://www.chinabusguide.com/
- https://www.trip.com/
- https://map.qq.com/
- https://www.travelchinaguide.com/
- Many foreign websites are blocked.
- Many historical places have been "renovated" in recent years into tourist traps devoid of authenticity.
- Must-try food: huoguo (hot pot)

ETHIOPIA
- Long-distance buses all leave around 5-6 AM. Better book 1-2 days in advance.
- Ethiopian 12-hour clock system follows sunrise and sunset (1 o'clock in the day = 7 AM, 12 o'clock at night = 6 AM), but the international time is sometimes used.
- Must hire a paid guide to visit national parks.
- Must-try food: injera, salads

EUROPE
- https://www.busradar.com/
- https://www.blablacar.com/
- http://www.azair.com/
- http://beta.map1.eu/
- https://www.megalithic.co.uk/
- https://maps.squat.net/en/cities
- https://ec.europa.eu/eurostat/cache/RCI/#?vis=nuts2.tourism&lang=en
- Western Europe claims to care about climate, but train travel is extremely expensive (maybe most in the world) while flying can be ridiculously cheap. Buses are reasonably priced.
- Cheapest airlines (Ryanair, Wizzair) have strict luggage rules and you must check in online or pay a huge fee.
- "Ethnological" museums, especially in London, Paris, Berlin, Brussels, and Vienna are full of stolen art from all over the world and reject the demands to return them.

GERMANY
- http://www.freepee.org/
- http://berlin-besetzt.de/
- 1-day train ticket can be a good deal for a small group (up to 5 people): https://www.bahn.com/en/view/offers/regional/day-ticket-for-germany.shtml

INDIA
- https://www.yatra.com/
- https://www.cleartrip.com/
- https://erail.in/
- https://www.irctc.co.in/nget/train-search
- Buying a train ticket is difficult: the queues are enormous, you need to fill out a complicated form, there are many price categories, tickets sell out early. Buy tickets online if possible. "Tatkal" tickets are more expensive but can be bought on day of departure. Buses are less confusing and more reliable.
- Food in India is often labeled with green (vegetarian) and brown (non-vegetarian) dot symbols. Wash your hands before eating. Eat with your right hand.
- Must-try food: thali, desserts, lassi

- https://www.pelni.co.id/

- Many mountains charge an enterance fee.

ISRAEL
- https://www.bus.co.il/otobusimmvc/en
- https://www.rail.co.il/en/
- Do not tell the border officials that you plan to visit Palestine or you will be searched and interrogated.
- The Palestinian Territories are essentially annexed by Israel (except Gaza). Palestinians are banned from some areas and roads. Gaza Strip is sealed off from outside world.
- There are young soldiers with machine guns everywhere.
- Must-try food: kanafeh

JAPAN
- https://world.jorudan.co.jp/mln/en/
- http://www.hyperdia.com/
- https://www.highwaybus.com/gp/inbound/index?lang=EN
- https://en.japantravel.com/japan-map
- https://japanhike.wordpress.com/
- http://best-hike-japan.com/
- The numerous railway and subway companies are incompatible with each other and use different tickets. Changing a subway line sometimes involves going out into the street and entering a different building.
- Public trash cans are very rare but every item you buy comes wrapped in plastic.
- Must-try food: natto, miso soup

KENYA
- https://metickets.krc.co.ke/
- The Nairobi-Mombasa train can get booked out long in advance but buses are very frequent.
- Must hire a paid guide to visit national parks.

MONGOLIA
- You can't cross the border on foot. You must catch a bus or train or hitchhike.
- Public transport exists pretty much only to/from Ulaanbaatar.

NEW ZEALAND
- http://www.topomap.co.nz/
- https://www.doc.govt.nz/map/index.html
- https://climbnz.org.nz/
- http://camping_nz.rankers.co.nz/filters/campgrounds
- Camping in national parks is allowed if you are at least 500 m away from a trail.
- "Great walks": The rangers will insist that you must complete them in several days and stay at the pre-booked huts, but it is perfectly legal to do the treks in a single day.
- Most free campsites and shelters are only for people who come in a big, polluting campervan. Hikers can get fined for staying there.

NORWAY
- https://www.norgeskart.no/
- You're allowed to camp almost anywhere.
- Some mountain cabins (hyttas) are free to use, but most are paid.

PERU
- https://www.redbus.pe/en/
- https://www.peru.travel/?internacional
- iPeru are the official tourist offices, provide free maps and useful information.
- Machu Picchu enterance tickets: https://www.machupicchu.gob.pe/inicio. Cheap transport option: bus Cusco to Hidroelectrica, then walk along the railway track to Aguas Calientes (main base for MP).
- Free and less touristy alternatives to the expensive Inca Trail: Lares trek, Salkantay trek, Choquequirao trek.
- Yurimaguas is the farthest place connected by road from where you can take a passenger boat to Iquitos and Brazil.

POLAND
- https://www.e-podroznik.pl/
- https://jakdojade.pl/
- Castles: https://zamki.res.pl/mapy.php
- Transit tickets phone app: Mobilet
- Different train types have different ticket offices.
- Many historic buildings were completely destroyed during World War 2. Most "old" buildings you see today are post-war restorations of varying accuracy.
- People seem to believe that "jaywalking" is a crime. The police loves fining pedestrians.
- Canteen: bar mleczny
