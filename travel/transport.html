BUS/MINIVAN
  • There are many different systems. Sometimes you pay online, sometimes at the ticket office, sometimes to the driver, sometimes to the conductor. Sometimes there is a main bus station, sometimes bus companies are scattered around the city. Sometimes there is a schedule, sometimes the buses only leave when full.
  • Usually you can hail down buses as they pass by, even where there is no bus stop.
  • The drivers might be competing with each other. They will rush you to their bus/minivan and then you have to wait forever for it to fill up with passengers. To avoid this, get inside buses that look almost full. If there are no passengers inside then it is unlikely to be leaving soon.
  • Don't get overcharged. If in doubt, ask other passengers what the price is.
TRAIN
  • In some countries you will have to show your passport when buying a ticket and again when boarding the train.
  • Rail passes are very popular among backpackers but in most cases they don't save any money.
SHARED TAXI
  • Common in many countries, it's like hitchhiking but you have to pay. Wave down a passing car or tricycle and say where you're going. Sometimes there is a fixed fare but usually you have to negotiate. The driver will make stops on the way to pick up more passengers. The drivers might also wait for passengers at set points, e.g. outside a train or bus station.
TAXI/MOTORBIKE TAXI
  • Always negotiate the price in advance.
  • Never put your bags in the trunk.
  • Don't trust the drivers. Better stick to public transport or walking or use ride-hailing apps.
HITCHHIKING
  • Wait at intersections or places where cars need to slow down. Make sure there is enough space for a car to stop.
  • It is often better to keep walking down the road instead of waiting in one spot. This signals the drivers that you're not a lazy bum but a serious person who needs to get somewhere and is putting in effort. If you walk long enough you might also reach an intersection with better traffic.
  • Be friendly and ready to chat with the drivers, they are often looking for a good conversation.
  • Expect to wait longer if you are two or more people, especially if you're both male.
  • Don't get in the car if you have a bad feeling about the driver. Make up some excuse (e.g. "I need to wait for my friend").
FLIGHTS
  • Buying tickets early doesn't always get a better price. Sometimes buying a few weeks or days before the flight can be cheaper.
  • To find a lower price, be flexible with the date and the airport. Consider breaking a longer trip into stages and search them separately.
  • Make yourself familiar with the routes of low-cost airlines, especially the long-haul ones. Some worth knowing are:
    Eurowings (Germany to N. America, Southern Africa and Thailand)
    Norwegian (Europe to the Americas and S-E Asia)
    TUI fly Belgium (Belgium to Africa and Caribbean)
    Rainbow Tours (Not an airline. Poland to S-E Asia, Africa, N. America, and Middle East)
    Wizz Air (E. Europe to Middle East and Kazakhstan)
    Ryanair (Europe to Middle East)
    Vueling (Spain to W. Africa and Middle East)
    Condor (Germany to the Americas, Africa, and S-E Asia)
    Thomas Cook (UK to N. America, Africa, and South Asia)
    Level (Europe to the Americas)
    XL Airways (France to N. America and Reunion)
    French Bee (France to N. America, Reunion, and Tahiti)
    Pegasus (Europe to Middle East via Istanbul)
    Pobeda (Europe and Middle East to Siberia via Moscow)
    WOW Air (N. America to Europe and India via Iceland)
    Scoot (Singapore to Europe, Australia, and Hawaii)
    Airasia (Malaysia to Australia and Hawaii)
    Cebu Pacific (Philippines to Middle East and Australia)
    Beijing Capital (China to Canada, Australia, and Europe)
    Jetstar (Australia to Asia and Hawaii)
    WestJet (Canada to Europe and C. America)
    Air Transat (Canada to Europe and C. America)
    Spirit (USA to South America)
  • I don't get the point of spending hundreds of dollars on more expensive flights that are "more comfortable". Would you buy a comfortable, 200$ chair if you can only sit in it for a few hours?
  • Airlines that provide more legroom fit fewer passengers into their planes, meaning that every passenger generates more carbon emissions. First class travel is the most harmful for the environment.
  • For better comfort, be the last person to board the plane. Sometimes you can find an empty row for yourself.
  • Avoid taking taxis directly from/to airports. If there is no decent public transport option, walk a few hundred meters out of the airport area and get a taxi or local bus from there.
